YOUR RIGHTS AS A NIGERIAN CITIZEN

A Comprehensive Guide

Understanding Your Constitutional Rights and How to Exercise Them

Published by:

INTRODUCTION: KNOW YOUR RIGHTS

As a Nigerian citizen, you are guaranteed certain fundamental rights by the 1999 Constitution of the Federal Republic of Nigeria (as amended). These rights are not privilegesâ€”they are your entitlements as a citizen, and understanding them is crucial for your protection and empowerment.

This guide will help you understand:

  • What rights you have as a Nigerian citizen
  • How to exercise these rights effectively
  • What to do when your rights are violated
  • How to seek redress and justice
Important: These rights apply to ALL Nigerian citizens regardless of tribe, religion, gender, or social status. No one can take these rights away from you.

1. RIGHT TO LIFE

Constitutional Provision: Section 33 - Every person has a right to life, and no one shall be deprived intentionally of his life.

What This Means:

  • Your life is sacred and protected by law
  • No individual, group, or authority can kill you unlawfully
  • The government must protect your life and safety
  • Law enforcement cannot use excessive force that could kill you
Real-Life Example: If police threaten to kill you during an arrest, they are violating your constitutional right to life. You can report this and seek legal action.

How to Exercise This Right:

  • Report threats to your life to the police immediately
  • Document any attempts on your life
  • Seek protection from law enforcement when threatened
  • Contact human rights organizations if needed

2. RIGHT TO DIGNITY AND RESPECT

Constitutional Provision: Section 34 - Every individual is entitled to respect for the dignity of his person.

What This Means:

  • No one can torture or treat you inhumanely
  • You cannot be held in slavery or forced labor
  • You must be treated with respect and dignity
  • Degrading treatment is prohibited
Real-Life Example: If police beat you during questioning or make you do unpaid work, they are violating your dignity. This is unconstitutional and punishable by law.

3. RIGHT TO PERSONAL LIBERTY

Constitutional Provision: Section 35 - Every person shall be entitled to his personal liberty.

What This Means:

  • You cannot be arrested or detained unlawfully
  • If arrested, you must be told why within 24 hours
  • You must be brought before a court within reasonable time
  • You have the right to bail in most cases
Real-Life Example: If police arrest you without telling you what you did wrong, or keep you for days without taking you to court, your right to personal liberty is being violated.

Important: When You Are Arrested

  • Ask: "What am I being arrested for?"
  • Request to call a lawyer or family member
  • Do not sign any document without understanding it
  • Remember: You have the right to remain silent

4. RIGHT TO FAIR HEARING

Constitutional Provision: Section 36 - In the determination of his civil rights and obligations, a person shall be entitled to a fair hearing.

What This Means:

  • You are innocent until proven guilty
  • You have the right to legal representation
  • Your case must be heard by an impartial court
  • You must be given adequate time to prepare your defense
  • Court proceedings must be public (with some exceptions)
Real-Life Example: If you are charged with a crime, the court cannot convict you without giving you a chance to defend yourself or without proper evidence.

5. RIGHT TO PRIVACY

Constitutional Provision: Section 37 - The privacy of citizens, their homes, correspondence, telephone conversations and telegraphic communications is hereby guaranteed and protected.

What This Means:

  • Your home cannot be searched without a warrant
  • Your phone calls and messages are private
  • Your personal information must be protected
  • Government cannot spy on you without legal basis
Real-Life Example: If police search your house without a warrant or proper legal authority, they are violating your right to privacy.

6. FREEDOM OF EXPRESSION AND PRESS

Constitutional Provision: Section 39 - Every person shall be entitled to freedom of expression, including freedom to hold opinions and to receive and impart ideas and information without interference.

What This Means:

  • You can express your opinions freely
  • You can criticize the government peacefully
  • You can share information and ideas
  • The media has the right to report news
  • You can write, speak, or publish your views
Real-Life Example: You can post on social media about government policies you disagree with. This is your constitutional right, and you cannot be arrested for peaceful criticism.
Note: This right has some limitsâ€”you cannot use it to incite violence, spread hate speech, or defame others.

7. FREEDOM OF ASSEMBLY AND ASSOCIATION

Constitutional Provision: Section 40 - Every person shall be entitled to assemble freely and associate with other persons.

What This Means:

  • You can organize or join peaceful protests
  • You can form or join political parties
  • You can join trade unions or professional associations
  • You can attend meetings and gatherings
Real-Life Example: You can organize a peaceful protest about bad roads in your community. Police cannot stop you if your protest is peaceful and lawful.

8. FREEDOM OF MOVEMENT

Constitutional Provision: Section 41 - Every citizen of Nigeria is entitled to move freely throughout Nigeria and to reside in any part thereof.

What This Means:

  • You can travel anywhere within Nigeria
  • You can live in any state or local government area
  • No one can prevent you from leaving Nigeria
  • You cannot be expelled from Nigeria
Real-Life Example: Even if you are from the North, you have the constitutional right to live and work in Lagos, Port Harcourt, or any other part of Nigeria.

9. FREEDOM FROM DISCRIMINATION

Constitutional Provision: Section 42 - A citizen of Nigeria shall not be subjected to disabilities or restrictions based on community, ethnic group, place of origin, sex, religion or political opinion.

What This Means:

  • You cannot be denied opportunities because of your tribe
  • Your religion should not prevent you from getting jobs
  • Women have equal rights with men
  • Your state of origin should not disadvantage you
Real-Life Example: If an employer refuses to hire you only because you are Igbo, Yoruba, Hausa, or from any particular tribe, this is unconstitutional discrimination.

10. RIGHT TO PROPERTY

Constitutional Provision: Section 43 - Every citizen of Nigeria shall have the right to acquire and own immovable property anywhere in Nigeria.

What This Means:

  • You can buy land anywhere in Nigeria
  • Your property cannot be taken without compensation
  • You have the right to own a house anywhere
  • Government acquisition must follow due process

WHAT TO DO WHEN YOUR RIGHTS ARE VIOLATED

Step 1: Document Everything

  • Write down what happened with dates and times
  • Take photos or videos if safe to do so
  • Get contact information of witnesses
  • Keep all relevant documents

Step 2: Report to Authorities

  • Police (if they are not the violators)
  • National Human Rights Commission
  • Public Complaints Commission
  • Legal Aid Council

Step 3: Seek Legal Help

  • Contact a lawyer immediately
  • Visit Legal Aid Council for free legal services
  • Contact civil society organizations
  • Approach the court for fundamental rights enforcement

Important Contact Numbers:

  • National Human Rights Commission: 09-4619500
  • Legal Aid Council: 08033142863
  • Emergency Services: 199, 911
  • Police Public Relations: 199

YOUR CIVIC RESPONSIBILITIES

While you have rights, you also have responsibilities as a Nigerian citizen:

Your Duties Include:

  • Respect the rights of other citizens
  • Obey the laws of Nigeria
  • Pay your taxes honestly
  • Vote in elections
  • Serve your community
  • Protect public property
  • Report crimes and corruption
  • Promote unity and peace
