HOW TO USE THESE TEMPLATES

Instructions:
  1. Print this document or copy the template you need
  2. Fill in your specific information where indicated
  3. Have documents signed and witnessed as required
  4. Make multiple copies for all parties involved
  5. Consider having complex documents reviewed by a lawyer
Important Disclaimer: These templates are for guidance only. For complex legal matters or significant transactions, always consult with a qualified Nigerian lawyer. Laws may vary by state.

AFFIDAVIT TEMPLATES

1. General Affidavit Template

AFFIDAVIT

IN THE _______ COURT OF _______

IN THE MATTER OF: _________________________________

I, [Your Full Name], male/female, aged [Age] years, occupation: [Occupation], residing at [Your Full Address] do hereby make oath and state as follows:

  1. That I am the deponent/applicant in this matter and I know the facts herein deposed to.
  2. That [State your facts clearly and numbered]
  3. That [Continue with additional facts]
  4. That I make this affidavit in good faith and for the purposes stated above.

SWORN TO at [Location]
This [Day] day of [Month, Year]

[Your Signature]
DEPONENT

BEFORE ME:
[Commissioner for Oaths/Notary Public Signature]
COMMISSIONER FOR OATHS/NOTARY PUBLIC

2. Affidavit of Loss of Document

AFFIDAVIT OF LOSS

I, [Your Full Name], do hereby make oath and state as follows:

  1. That I am the lawful owner/holder of [Document Type] bearing number [Document Number] issued on [Date].
  2. That on or about [Date of Loss], I discovered that the said document was missing.
  3. That I have conducted diligent search for the document but it cannot be found.
  4. That I believe the document was lost through [Circumstances - theft/misplacement/etc.].
  5. That I have reported the loss to the appropriate authorities.
  6. That if the original document is found, I will return any replacement issued to me.

[Signature and Date]
DEPONENT

ADDITIONAL RESOURCES

Where to Get Forms Processed:
  • Affidavits: Any Commissioner for Oaths, Notary Public, or Magistrate
  • Statutory Declarations: Commissioner for Oaths or Magistrate
  • Probate Matters: High Court Probate Registry
  • Business Registration: Corporate Affairs Commission (CAC)
  • Land Matters: Land Registry in your state
Important Notes:
  • Always bring valid identification when processing legal documents
  • Keep copies of all signed documents
  • Witness signatures should be from adults who are not parties to the document
  • Some documents may require specific stamps or fees
  • When in doubt, consult with a qualified lawyer
