INTRODUCTION: YOUR ROLE IN BUILDING NIGERIA

Citizenship is not just about enjoying rights‚Äîit comes with responsibilities. As a Nigerian citizen, you have important duties that help build a strong, united, and prosperous nation. Your active participation in civic life is essential for the development of our democracy and the progress of our country.

This guide outlines your civic duties and shows you practical ways to fulfill them. Remember: A responsible citizen makes a responsible nation.

"Ask not what your country can do for you‚Äîask what you can do for your country." - Adapted for Nigeria

1. OBEY THE LAWS OF NIGERIA

Your Duty: As a citizen, you must obey all laws made by legitimate authorities‚Äîfederal, state, and local government laws.

What This Means:

  • Follow traffic laws and regulations
  • Respect property rights of others
  • Do not engage in criminal activities
  • Comply with business and professional regulations
  • Respect court orders and judgments
Practical Actions:
  • Always carry valid identification documents
  • Drive with a valid license and vehicle papers
  • Pay for goods and services honestly
  • Respect "No Smoking" and public safety signs
  • Report crimes you witness to authorities

Why This Matters:

When citizens obey laws, society becomes safer, more predictable, and more prosperous. Law-abiding citizens create an environment where businesses can thrive and individuals can pursue their dreams safely.

2. PAY YOUR TAXES HONESTLY

Your Duty: Pay all taxes owed to federal, state, and local governments honestly and on time.

Types of Taxes You Must Pay:

  • Personal Income Tax: On your salary and business income
  • Value Added Tax (VAT): On goods and services you buy
  • Property Tax: On land and buildings you own
  • Business Taxes: If you own a business
  • Vehicle Registration: Annual vehicle licensing
How to Fulfill This Duty:
  • Obtain a Tax Identification Number (TIN)
  • File your tax returns annually
  • Keep proper records of your income and expenses
  • Use receipts that show VAT payments
  • Register your business properly
  • Report tax evasion when you see it

Why This Matters:

Taxes fund public services like roads, schools, hospitals, security, and infrastructure. When you pay taxes, you contribute to building the Nigeria you want to see. Tax evasion hurts everyone by reducing funds available for development.

3. VOTE IN ELECTIONS

Your Duty: Participate in the democratic process by voting in elections and referendums.

Elections You Should Vote In:

  • Presidential Elections: Choose Nigeria's President
  • National Assembly Elections: Choose Senators and House of Reps members
  • Gubernatorial Elections: Choose your state governor
  • State Assembly Elections: Choose state lawmakers
  • Local Government Elections: Choose LGA chairmen and councilors
Steps to Fulfill This Duty:
  • Register to vote with INEC (get your PVC)
  • Research candidates and their policies
  • Vote on election day
  • Stay and witness vote counting if possible
  • Report electoral malpractices
  • Encourage others to vote

Why This Matters:

Voting is your primary way to choose leaders and influence government policies. When you don't vote, you give up your power to shape Nigeria's future. Bad leaders often win when good citizens don't vote.

4. SERVE YOUR COMMUNITY

Your Duty: Contribute to the development and well-being of your community through service and participation.

Ways to Serve Your Community:

  • Participate in community meetings and decision-making
  • Volunteer for community development projects
  • Join or support community organizations
  • Help maintain cleanliness in your area
  • Support community security initiatives
  • Mentor young people in your community
Practical Examples:
  • Join your local Community Development Association (CDA)
  • Participate in monthly environmental sanitation
  • Volunteer at local schools or health centers
  • Organize skill training for unemployed youth
  • Support community vigilante groups
  • Contribute to community projects (roads, water, electricity)

5. PROTECT PUBLIC PROPERTY

Your Duty: Safeguard and protect public property and infrastructure from damage, theft, or misuse.

Public Property Includes:

  • Roads, bridges, and traffic lights
  • Public schools and hospitals
  • Government buildings and offices
  • Parks and recreational facilities
  • Street lights and public utilities
  • Public transport vehicles and facilities
How to Protect Public Property:
  • Don't vandalize or damage public facilities
  • Report vandalism and theft to authorities
  • Use public facilities responsibly
  • Join community efforts to maintain public property
  • Educate others about protecting public property
  • Challenge those who damage public facilities

Why This Matters:

Public property belongs to all Nigerians. When it's destroyed, taxpayers' money is wasted on repairs instead of new development. Protecting public property saves money and ensures everyone can enjoy these facilities.

6. PROMOTE UNITY AND TOLERANCE

Your Duty: Foster national unity by respecting diversity and promoting peaceful coexistence among all Nigerians.

Ways to Promote Unity:

  • Respect people from different tribes and religions
  • Learn about other Nigerian cultures
  • Speak against tribal and religious discrimination
  • Build friendships across ethnic and religious lines
  • Promote intermarriage and cultural exchange
  • Use unifying language in public discourse
Practical Steps:
  • Learn to greet people in different Nigerian languages
  • Attend cultural festivals from other tribes
  • Defend people being discriminated against
  • Share positive stories about other ethnic groups
  • Correct stereotypes and misconceptions when you hear them
  • Support policies that promote national integration

7. FIGHT CORRUPTION

Your Duty: Refuse to engage in corruption and actively work to eliminate it from our society.

Forms of Corruption to Avoid:

  • Paying bribes to government officials
  • Accepting bribes if you're a public officer
  • Using your position for personal gain
  • Embezzling public funds
  • Inflating contracts or invoices
  • Exam malpractice and certificate forgery
How to Fight Corruption:
  • Refuse to pay or accept bribes
  • Report corruption to EFCC, ICPC, or other agencies
  • Demand receipts for all government payments
  • Support transparency initiatives
  • Vote for honest leaders
  • Educate others about the dangers of corruption

Reporting Corruption:

  • EFCC: 08093322644, info@efccnigeria.org
  • ICPC: 08076369259, complaints@icpc.gov.ng
  • Code of Conduct Bureau: 09-5234785
  • Public Complaints Commission: 09-6708614

8. DEFEND THE NATION

Your Duty: Contribute to national defense and security through various means, not just military service.

Ways to Defend Nigeria:

  • Report suspicious activities to security agencies
  • Provide information to help fight crime and terrorism
  • Support security personnel in their duties
  • Participate in community policing initiatives
  • Avoid actions that threaten national security
  • Promote peace and conflict resolution
Emergency Numbers for Reporting:
  • Police: 199
  • Emergency Services: 911
  • DSS: 08132222105
  • NEMA: 0700-10-10-10

9. RESPECT THE RIGHTS OF OTHERS

Your Duty: Respect and protect the fundamental rights of fellow citizens just as you expect yours to be respected.

Rights of Others to Respect:

  • Right to life and personal safety
  • Right to freedom of expression and religion
  • Right to property and privacy
  • Right to freedom from discrimination
  • Right to due process and fair treatment
How to Respect Others' Rights:
  • Don't threaten or harm anyone
  • Allow others to express their views peacefully
  • Respect people's property and privacy
  • Treat everyone equally regardless of background
  • Support fair treatment for all
  • Speak up when you see rights violations

10. PURSUE EDUCATION AND DEVELOPMENT

Your Duty: Continuously educate yourself and contribute to the intellectual and social development of the nation.

Areas for Personal Development:

  • Formal education and skill acquisition
  • Civic education and constitutional awareness
  • Professional and vocational training
  • Digital literacy and technology skills
  • Environmental and health awareness
  • Financial literacy and entrepreneurship
Ways to Fulfill This Duty:
  • Complete your basic education
  • Learn new skills throughout your life
  • Read newspapers and stay informed
  • Attend civic education programs
  • Teach others what you know
  • Support education in your community

THE REWARDS OF GOOD CITIZENSHIP

Personal Benefits:

  • Sense of purpose and belonging
  • Respect from your community
  • Better living environment
  • Improved security and safety
  • Access to better public services

National Benefits:

  • Stronger democracy and institutions
  • Economic growth and development
  • Social cohesion and unity
  • International respect and recognition
  • Peaceful and prosperous society
