UNDERSTANDING NIGERIAN COURTS

Court Hierarchy

  1. Supreme Court: Highest court in Nigeria (final appeals)
  2. Court of Appeal: Intermediate appellate court
  3. Federal High Court: Federal matters and constitutional issues
  4. State High Court: State matters and serious criminal/civil cases
  5. Magistrate Court: Minor criminal and civil matters
  6. Customary/Area Courts: Traditional and religious matters

Which Court to Use?

FILING A CIVIL CASE

Before You Start

  • Try alternative dispute resolution (mediation, arbitration)
  • Gather all relevant documents and evidence
  • Determine if your case is within the limitation period
  • Consider the cost versus potential recovery

Step 1: Prepare Your Documents

Required Documents:
  • Writ of Summons or Originating Summons
  • Statement of Claim
  • Affidavit in support (if required)
  • All supporting documents
  • Legal fee payment receipt

Step 2: File at Court Registry

  1. Visit the appropriate court registry
  2. Pay prescribed filing fees
  3. Submit original and required copies
  4. Obtain court seal and case number
  5. Get hearing date from court

Step 3: Serve the Defendant

Service Methods:
  • Personal service by court bailiff
  • Service through recognized agents
  • Substituted service (with court permission)
  • Service by publication (in extreme cases)

Step 4: Case Management and Trial

  1. Attend case management conference
  2. Exchange pleadings with other parties
  3. Conduct pre-trial conferences
  4. Present your case during trial
  5. Await judgment

CRIMINAL COURT PROCEDURES

If You Are Arrested

Your Rights:
  • Right to remain silent
  • Right to legal representation
  • Right to be informed of charges
  • Right to be brought before court within 24-48 hours
  • Right to bail (except in capital offenses)

Criminal Trial Process

  1. Charge Reading: Charges are read to you in court
  2. Plea Taking: You enter guilty or not guilty plea
  3. Bail Application: Application for bail if applicable
  4. Case Management: Court sets hearing dates
  5. Trial: Prosecution presents case, defense responds
  6. Judgment: Court delivers verdict
  7. Sentencing: If found guilty, court imposes sentence

Filing a Criminal Complaint

Steps to Report a Crime:
  1. Report to nearest police station
  2. Obtain police incident report number
  3. Provide detailed statement
  4. Submit supporting evidence
  5. Follow up on investigation
  6. Cooperate with prosecution if case goes to court

COMMON COURT DOCUMENTS

Civil Case Documents

  • Writ of Summons: Document starting a civil case
  • Statement of Claim: Details of your case
  • Statement of Defense: Defendant's response
  • Affidavit: Sworn statement of facts
  • Motion: Request for court order
  • Judgment: Court's final decision

Criminal Case Documents

  • Charge Sheet: List of criminal charges
  • Information: Formal accusation document
  • Plea: Defendant's response to charges
  • Bail Bond: Document securing temporary release
  • Conviction: Finding of guilt
  • Sentence: Punishment imposed by court

APPEALING A COURT DECISION

When Can You Appeal?

  • Dissatisfied with court judgment
  • Believe there was error in law or procedure
  • New evidence has emerged
  • Sentence is too harsh (criminal cases)

Appeal Process

  1. File Notice of Appeal: Within 30 days of judgment
  2. Obtain Court Records: Get certified copies of proceedings
  3. Prepare Appeal Brief: State grounds for appeal
  4. Pay Appeal Fees: As prescribed by court rules
  5. Serve Other Parties: Notify respondents
  6. Attend Hearing: Present oral arguments
  7. Await Decision: Court of Appeal judgment
Important: Appeals must be filed within strict time limits. Missing deadlines may result in loss of appeal rights.

COURT ETIQUETTE AND CONDUCT

How to Dress

  • Wear formal, conservative clothing
  • Men: Long trousers, shirt, tie (jacket preferred)
  • Women: Modest dress or suit, covered shoulders
  • Remove hats and caps in courtroom
  • Avoid flashy jewelry and casual wear

Court Behavior

  • Arrive early and be punctual
  • Stand when judge enters/leaves
  • Address judge as "Your Lordship/Worship"
  • Speak only when addressed
  • Turn off mobile phones
  • No eating, drinking, or chewing gum
  • Listen carefully and follow instructions
