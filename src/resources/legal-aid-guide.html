WHAT IS LEGAL AID?

Legal aid is free or low-cost legal assistance provided to citizens who cannot afford to hire private lawyers. In Nigeria, legal aid is guaranteed by the Constitution and provided through various government and non-governmental organizations.

Your Right to Legal Aid: Section 36(6)(c) of the 1999 Constitution guarantees that every citizen charged with a criminal offense has the right to be provided with legal aid free of charge by the state.

LEGAL AID COUNCIL OF NIGERIA

About Legal Aid Council

The Legal Aid Council of Nigeria is the primary government agency responsible for providing free legal services to indigent Nigerians. Established by the Legal Aid Act, the Council operates offices nationwide.

Who Qualifies for Legal Aid?

  1. Income Test: Your monthly income must not exceed the current minimum wage
  2. Means Test: You must demonstrate financial inability to hire a private lawyer
  3. Merit Test: Your case must have reasonable chances of success
  4. Special Categories: Automatic qualification for:
    • Persons with disabilities
    • Children and juveniles
    • Women in certain circumstances
    • Elderly persons (65 years and above)

Types of Cases Covered

  • Criminal defense (all criminal charges)
  • Land and property disputes
  • Family law matters (divorce, custody, maintenance)
  • Employment and labor disputes
  • Human rights violations
  • Consumer protection issues
  • Immigration and citizenship matters
  • Administrative law disputes

HOW TO APPLY FOR LEGAL AID

Step 1: Visit the Nearest Legal Aid Office

Locate the Legal Aid Council office nearest to you. They have offices in all 36 states and the Federal Capital Territory.

Step 2: Complete Application Form

Fill out the Legal Aid Application Form (Form LAC 001). You can get this form at any Legal Aid office or download it from their website.

Step 3: Provide Required Documents

  • Valid means of identification (National ID, Voter's Card, Driver's License, or Passport)
  • Proof of income (salary slip, bank statement, or affidavit of income)
  • All documents related to your case
  • Police report (for criminal cases)
  • Medical report (if applicable)

Step 4: Interview and Assessment

A legal aid officer will interview you to assess your case and determine eligibility. Be honest and provide all relevant information.

Step 5: Case Assignment

If approved, your case will be assigned to a legal aid lawyer who will represent you throughout the legal process.

Many Nigerian universities with law faculties operate legal aid clinics that provide free legal services to the public. Contact law faculties in your area.
